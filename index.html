ePCR Documentation Tips

General Principles

  • Be Objective: Document facts, not opinions. Use "patient states" instead of assumptions.
  • Be Accurate: Record exact times, measurements, and observations.
  • Be Complete: Document all relevant findings, even if normal.
  • Be Timely: Complete the PCR as soon as possible after the call.
  • Be Professional: Use proper medical terminology and maintain patient dignity.

Documentation Mnemonics

  • CHART: Chief complaint, History, Assessment, Rx/Treatment, Transport
  • OPQRST: Onset, Provocation, Quality, Radiation, Severity, Time
  • SAMPLE: Signs/Symptoms, Allergies, Medications, Past history, Last oral intake, Events
  • DCAP-BTLS: Deformities, Contusions, Abrasions, Punctures, Burns, Tenderness, Lacerations, Swelling

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using vague terms like "normal" without specific findings
  • Forgetting to document patient refusals or non-compliance
  • Omitting reassessment findings after interventions
  • Using abbreviations that aren't standard or approved
  • Failing to document exact times for critical events
  • Not documenting patient's own words (use quotes)

Legal Considerations

  • If it's not documented, it didn't happen (from legal perspective)
  • Never alter or destroy a PCR once completed
  • Document refusal of care with witness signatures when possible
  • Be honest about errors or adverse events
  • Maintain patient confidentiality (HIPAA compliance)
